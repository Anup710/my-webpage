The whore who took my poems



    Some say we should keep personal remorse from the

    poem

    stay abstract and there is some reason in this,

    but jezus; 

    twelve poems gone and I don't keep carbons

    and you have

    my paintings too, my best ones; its stifling

    are you trying to crush me out like the rest of them?

    Why didn't you take my money? they usually do

    from the sleeping drunken pants sick in the corner.

    Next time, take my left arms

    or a fify

    but not my poems. 

    I'm not Shakesepare 

    but sometime simply 

    there won't be any more, abstract or otherwise. 

    There will always will money and whores and drunkards

    down to the last bomb, 

    as God said,

    crossing his legs, 

    I see I have made plenty many poets,

    but not so very much 

    poetry.
- Charles Bukowski
